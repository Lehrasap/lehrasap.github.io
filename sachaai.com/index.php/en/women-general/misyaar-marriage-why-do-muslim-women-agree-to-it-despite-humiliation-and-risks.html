Why do women still agree to Misyaar Marriage despite humiliation? (The same REASONS as those are present in Mut'ah Marriage)

The simple answer is that the Islamic system often makes women so helpless in many situations that they are left with no other option but to accept their humiliation through Mut'ah and Misyaar.

They are not only confined in their Burqas, but they are practically restricted to their houses, and it is discouraged for them to leave the four walls of their homes. Even if they do go outside, it is only in the company of their guardians (Mahram males).

All this results in extreme financial vulnerability for women, making it impossible for them to survive independently. Thus, they are practically forced to seek full or partial support from men to survive, especially if they have passed the age of 30 or are widowed or divorced (and have children). This financial vulnerability of poor women provides men with ample opportunity to blackmail them into entering such exploitative relationships, where men do not have to give women the full rights they deserve as wives.

Shia Muslim women find themselves compelled to accept the humiliation of Mut'ah marriage in these situations, while Sunni Muslim women are compelled to accept the humiliation of Misyaar marriage.

  • Or they also get the option not to marry at all to avoid the humiliation of Mut'ah or Misyaar, if they are financially stable enough to support themselves and their children.

 

Misyaar Marriage cannot be declared Haram while it roots lie directly in the Quran and Muhammad himself practiced it:

Muslim scholars are fully aware of the harmful consequences of Misyaar marriage. They ensure that their own sisters, daughters, and mothers do not engage in it, and they issue numerous warnings to other Muslim women against participating in Misyaar marriages (source). However, they are unable to declare it Haram (forbidden) and openly prohibit it because the roots of Misyaar are found directly in the Quran, and Muhammad himself practiced it.

It all started when Muhammad grew stronger and wealthier in the later stage of the Medinan period. He began marrying multiple young and beautiful women, despite his own age ranging between 58 and 63 years. The women he married were generally between the ages of 17 and 35, making Muhammad double, triple, or even four times their age. Islamic traditions highlight that these women, such as Juwayria, Safiyyah, Rehana, Zaynab, and Umm Habiba, were not only young but also exceptionally beautiful.

However, Sawdah was the only wife of Muhammad who was older than the newlywed ladies, though still younger than Muhammad himself. In the presence of his young and attractive wives, Muhammad desired to part ways with Sawdah. Muhammad had married Sawdah in Mecca during a time of weakness and poverty when he faced opposition from the entire city.

To fulfill his wish of separating from Sawdah, Muhammad once again invoked revelation and claimed that Allah had sent the following verse:

(Quran 33:51) You, [O Muhammad], may put aside whom you will of them or take to yourself whom you will.

Therefore, after the revelation of this verse, the only wife whom Muhammad chose to divorce, was the old lady Sawdah. It was despite the fact that Sawdah was serving Muhammad from the time of Mecca, when Muhammad was poor and weak, while other wives were new, and they hardly lived with Muhammad for a few years in Medina. 

According to Tafsir Ibn Kathir (Verse 4:128), upon learning of Muhammad's intention to divorce her, Sawdah sought refuge in 'Aisha's house. When Muhammad arrived, Sawdah expressed her willingness to give up her turn to 'Aisha but pleaded with him not to divorce her. Muhammad readily agreed and promptly claimed the revelation of the following verse:

(Quran 4:128-129) And if a woman fears from her husband contempt or evasion, there is no sin upon them if they make terms of settlement between them (i.e. woman agrees upon leaving some of her rights) … And you will never be able to do Justice (Arabic: تَعْدِلُوْا) between wives, even if you should strive [to do so].

Furthermore, do you notice the contradiction? Previously, the author of the Quran (Muhammad) had established the condition of justice ('adl) for having multiple wives (Quran 4:3). However, in this particular verse (Quran 4:128-129), he permits a husband to manipulate his wives by threatening divorce, thus pressuring them into relinquishing their rights in the name of a settlement. This undermines the requirement of justice, as the husband retains control over the right to divorce, resulting in settlements that consistently favor him.

(Quran 4:3) If ye fear that ye shall not be able to deal justly with the orphans, marry women of your choice, Two or three or four; but if ye fear that ye shall not be able to deal justly (with them), then only one, or (a captive) that your right hands possess, that will be more suitable, to prevent you from doing injustice.

Practically, the verse (Quran 4:128-129) has abrogated the verse of doing justice among multiple wives.

This action of Muhammad, and this verse of the Quran that granted Muhammad his wish, is the base upon which the whole institution of Misyaar marriage was later constructed. And this is the reason that Muslim Scholars are completely unable to declare Misyaar Haram, while its root lies directly in the Quran and Muhammad's action.
