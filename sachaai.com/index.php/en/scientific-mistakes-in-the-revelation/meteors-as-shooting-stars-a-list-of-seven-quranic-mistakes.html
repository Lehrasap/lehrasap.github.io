Details
Written by: Lehrasap
Category: Scientific Mistakes in the Revelation

Summary:

The Quran contains many scientific errors regarding the nature of meteors, also known as shooting stars. According to the Quran:

  1. It describes the existence of seven heavens, with the lowest 1st heaven being closest to Earth.
  2. The stars (which appear STATIC to us) are depicted as 'burning lamps' as they are visible to us emitting light. They are placed under the lowest 1st heaven of the Earth to adorn it.
  3. The Quran claims that these same stars  (which are static in their normal state and serve as lamps) transform into SHOOTING stars (i.e. meterors) when angels throw these static stars as missiles to attack the devils who try to listen to Allah's commandments in the first heaven.

However, modern scientific knowledge contradicts this Quranic account:

  1. Lamps like static stars don't turn into shooting stars. Stars are massive, self-luminous celestial bodies made of gas, emitting light and heat through nuclear fusion.
  2. But the shooting stars are asteroids, which are rocky objects, primarily found between Mars and Jupiter in the asteroid belt.
  3. These asteroids are not burning and emitting lights as 'burning lamps'. And they are also not adorning the sky. No, but they are dark, and cold and totally invisible to our human eye. 
  4. When asteroids collide, they break into smaller fragments called meteoroids. When these meteoroids enter Earth’s atmosphere, they burn up due to friction and appear as meteors.

The next clash between the Quran and science is that the author of the Quran was unaware of the existence of dark stars along with asteroids:

  • In space, aside from burning stars like the sun, there are trillions of solid stars similar to our Earth, but they don't appear twinkling to us; rather, they are completely dark and invisible to the human eye.
  • Apart from burning stars like the sun, we can only see a few nearby planets, such as Mars and Jupiter, which twinkle because they reflect sunlight.
  • However, the trillions of stars that do not burn like the sun remain invisible to us, and they do not adorn the sky because their distance from Earth is so great that their reflected light cannot reach us.

However, the author of the Quran (i.e. Muhammad himself) had no knowledge of these trillions of dark stars, along with asteroids. Instead, according to the Quran, all stars in the sky are twinkling because Allah created all of them to adorn the world’s first heaven with their brightness.

  • Prior to their atmospheric entry, meteoroids do travel like missiles towards the earth due to its gravitational power, but without emitting any fire or light or flame.
  • They also don't serve as adornments for the sky; in fact, they remain dark and imperceptible to the naked eye.
