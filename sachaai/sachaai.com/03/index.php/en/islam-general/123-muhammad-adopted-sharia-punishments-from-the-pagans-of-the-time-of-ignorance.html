Muhammad adopted Sharia Punishments from the Pagans of the Time of Ignorance

In Islamic Sharia laws, there is an absence of imprisonment as a punishment for any crime. Instead, all punishments are physical, ranging from lashes to hanging, stoning, amputations, and beheading.

Why is this the case?

The explanation is straightforward. During Muhammad's era, the Arab society was considered ignorant and uncivilized, with no established prison system. Consequently, punishments had to be administered immediately, and the only feasible method was through physical penalties for all crimes.

Given the absence of an omniscient deity like Allah, it was Muhammad who formulated the Sharia punishments. Thus, he mirrored the mentality of his contemporary society, which was both ignorant and uncivilized, resulting in the imposition of various severe physical punishments.

In the commentary on the Quranic verse concerning theft (Verse 5:38), Ibn Kathir affirmed that such practices, including hand amputation, were prevalent in pre-Islamic Arab society and were incorporated into Islam. He wrote (link):

"During the time of Jahiliyyah, this (cutting of hands) was also the punishment for the thief, and Islam upheld this punishment."

It appears that Muhammad initially mandated hand amputation for even minor thefts, such as an egg or a rope (Reference: Sahih Bukhari, 6799). However, he later revised this to apply to thefts exceeding a value of 1/4th of a Dinar. This was the sole modification he made to the pre-Islamic laws. It is noteworthy that Muslim scholars are not unanimous on this change, with some believing that hand amputation should apply to any theft, regardless of value.

To provide context, a cloak cost 3 Dinars (i.e., 30 Dirhams) during Muhammad's era (Sunan Abu Dawud, 4394). Therefore, stealing goods worth 1/12th of a cloak's value was sufficient to warrant hand amputation.
